## About This Project

This repository supports the independent review paper on heavy metal contamination in groundwater across Punjab, Pakistan. The paper synthesizes literature from 2018–2026, evaluates health risks, and examines nanotechnology-based remediation strategies.

I wrote this review because I live in Faisalabad, Punjab. In my village and the surrounding areas, groundwater is not drinkable. It stains clothes. It tastes metallic. People have grown used to it, but they shouldn't have to. As a chemistry teacher, I see students who struggle with concentration — and I know the science. Lead reduces IQ. Arsenic affects cognitive development. This is not abstract. It is daily life here.

This project is my contribution to understanding and solving this crisis.

## Key Findings

- **20–30%** of Punjab's water resources exceed WHO arsenic limits
- **Kasur district** recorded arsenic at **2,046 μg/L** — 200× the WHO limit
- **Nanoadsorbents** achieve **>90% removal** for multiple heavy metals
- **Green synthesis** using agricultural waste offers affordable solutions for rural Pakistan
- **Machine learning** is emerging as a tool for optimizing nanomaterial design

---

## Why This Matters

Punjab is Pakistan's most populous province. Over **127 million people** live here. Most rely on groundwater for drinking. When that water is poisoned by heavy metals, the health burden is catastrophic:

- Cancer risks from arsenic and chromium
- Neurological damage in children from lead
- Kidney disease from cadmium
- Economic costs running into billions of rupees

Conventional treatment fails. Nanotechnology offers hope — but only if we can make it scalable, affordable, and safe.
